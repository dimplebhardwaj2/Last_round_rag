# LAST ROUND Evaluation Rubric

This rubric controls how LAST ROUND should judge interviews. The grader must be direct, evidence-based, and strict. It must never reward a candidate for skills that are not demonstrated in the transcript.

## Core Principle

Score only what the candidate actually showed.

Do not infer competence from confidence, politeness, role title, seniority, resume claims, or the fact that the candidate attempted the interview. If the transcript does not contain enough evidence, the score must be low.

## Non-Evidence Answers

Treat an answer as non-evidence when it is empty, evasive, unrelated, or too thin to judge.

Examples:

- "no answer"
- "no"
- "I don't know"
- "not sure"
- "skip"
- "pass"
- "nothing"
- gibberish or random characters
- one-word or very short responses with no reasoning
- generic filler that does not answer the question

If most answers are non-evidence, the interview cannot receive a decent rating.

## Score Caps

These caps are mandatory:

- If there are no meaningful candidate answers, maximum overall score is 1.
- If most answers are non-evidence, maximum overall score is 2.
- If most answers are vague or generic, maximum overall score is 3.
- A score of 4 requires multiple concrete, relevant answers with examples, reasoning, or trade-offs.
- A score of 5 requires consistently strong answers with clear structure, depth, specificity, and role-level judgment.

The backend may enforce these caps after the LLM returns its report.

## Strengths

Strengths must be earned. Only list a strength if the transcript directly proves it.

Bad strength:

- "Good communication skills" when the candidate gave short or vague answers.

Better strength:

- "Clarified scale and latency requirements before proposing a system design."

If no real strength is demonstrated, use:

"No clear strengths demonstrated from the provided answers."

## Improvements

Improvements should be brutally honest but professional.

Good improvement feedback should identify exactly what was missing:

- The candidate did not provide enough substance to evaluate ability.
- The answer lacked concrete examples, trade-offs, or reasoning.
- The candidate should explain their role, actions, decisions, and outcomes.
- The candidate should avoid saying "no answer" or skipping questions in a real interview.

Do not soften weak performance with vague encouragement.

## Criteria

Criteria should reflect the selected interview type, but each criterion must cite transcript evidence.

Common criteria:

- Relevance
- Depth of reasoning
- Communication clarity
- Role-level judgment
- Technical accuracy
- Behavioral specificity
- Trade-off analysis

When evidence is missing, the criterion score should usually be 1 or 2.

## Model Answer

The model answer should show what a strong response could have looked like. It should be concise and relevant to one of the main questions asked.
